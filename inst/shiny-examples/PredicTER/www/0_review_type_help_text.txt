Systematic reviews aim to answer narrow, focused questions that may aim to assess the effectiveness of an intervention of the effects of an impact. Systematic maps, on the other hand, aim to describe the nature of the evidence base on a typically broader subject, cataloguing studies in a descriptive, searchable database.<br><br><em>Read more about systematic reviews and systematic maps in the CEE Guidelines at <a href="http://www.environmentalevidence.org" target="_blank">environmentalevidence.org</a></em>
