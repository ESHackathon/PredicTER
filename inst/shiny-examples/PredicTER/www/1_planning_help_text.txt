The planning stage of a systematic review or map is where the scope and nature of the evidence synthesis project is decided upon, typically setting out the methods in a peer-reviewed protocol document. A key decision to make is how much planning time you need, and what resources can be committed to the project (lead staff FTE). It is also important to consider what proportion of the lead reviewer's time is likely to be spent on administration.<br><br><em>Read more about planning in the CEE Guidelines at <a href="http://www.environmentalevidence.org" target="_blank">environmentalevidence.org</a></em>
