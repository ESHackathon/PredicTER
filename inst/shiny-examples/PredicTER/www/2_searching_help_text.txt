Searching for evidence is arguably the most important stage to get right in a review. Multiple academic bibliographic databases should be searched using a tried and tested search string. Searches for grey literature should also be performed to obtain non-academic literature (like government or NGO reports) and to counteract publication bias. Duplicates will need to be removed before the unique articles are taken forward to the screening stage.<br><br><em>Read more about searching in the CEE Guidelines at <a href="http://www.environmentalevidence.org" target="_blank">environmentalevidence.org</a></em>
