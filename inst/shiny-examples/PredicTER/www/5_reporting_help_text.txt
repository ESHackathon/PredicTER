During the reporting stage, the conduct of the review should be outlined in detail to allow full repeatability of the methods used, along with highlighting the results of the synthesis. These stages can often be roughly estimated. Time spent on communicating the review may include the production of factsheets, policy briefs, social media materials, etc. but may be excluded from the calculation if communications budgets are external to the review process.<br><br><em>Read more about reporting in the CEE Guidelines at <a href="http://www.environmentalevidence.org" target="_blank">environmentalevidence.org</a></em>
